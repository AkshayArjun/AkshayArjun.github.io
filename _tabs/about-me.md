# Hi, I'm Akshay! 👋

I am a final-year undergraduate at **BITS Pilani, Hyderabad**, where I've spent the last few years obsessed with the math and mechanics that make things move autonomously. My journey in robotics is driven by a simple goal: bridging the gap between high-level learning-based intelligence and the rigorous safety guarantees of classical control theory.

Currently, I'm at **IISc Bangalore** (FOCAS Lab) working on my undergraduate thesis. My research here focuses on **Formal Methods in Control**, where I'm exploring ways to mathematically verify that an autonomous system will behave exactly how we intend it to, even in complex environments. 

Before coming to IISc, I spent time at the **Robotics Research Center (RRC) at IIIT Hyderabad**. Working under Prof. Spandan Roy, I developed a framework for **agile drone flight** using Reinforcement Learning. We used ROS 2 and Gazebo to push the limits of what these drones can do in simulation, focusing on high-speed maneuvers that traditional controllers often struggle with. 

Beyond drones, I've spent a lot of time thinking about bio-inspired robotics—specifically **snake robot locomotion**—and the unique challenges of nonlinear control in platforms like **bicopters**. Whether it's designing a flight controller PCB at CSIR-CEERI or leading the Automation and Robotics Club at BITS, I love the process of taking a theoretical control law and seeing it come to life in hardware.
